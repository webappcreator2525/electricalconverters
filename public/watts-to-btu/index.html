How to convert Watts to BTU/hr

The formula is BTU/hr = Watts × 3.412142.

For example, a 1500 W space heater will output approximately 5118 BTU/hr. Resistive electric heating is 100% efficient, so the electrical watts equal the thermal watts output.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why convert watts to BTU?

BTU is the standard measurement for HVAC systems in the United States, so converting electrical power to BTU helps you size a space heater or understand the cooling equivalent of your equipment.

Are all watts converted to heat?

For a purely resistive electric heater, yes, essentially 100% of the electrical watts are converted to heat (thermal watts), which equals 3.412 BTU/hr per watt.

Is a BTU the same as a ton of AC?

No, but they measure the same thing. One "ton" of refrigeration capacity is equal to 12,000 BTU/hr.
