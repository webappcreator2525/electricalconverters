Formula: W = BTU/hr ÷ 3.412142

How to convert BTU to Watts

The formula is Watts = BTU/hr / 3.412142.

For example, a typical 12000 BTU/hr window air conditioner is equivalent to approximately 3517 W of thermal cooling power. This represents the heat removed, not necessarily the electrical draw.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is this the power my AC draws from the wall?

No. A 12,000 BTU/hr AC produces 3517 W of cooling power, but due to the refrigeration cycle (EER/SEER rating), it might only consume 1000 W to 1200 W of electrical power from your outlet.

What does BTU stand for?

British Thermal Unit. It is defined as the amount of heat required to raise the temperature of one pound of water by one degree Fahrenheit.

What is the exact conversion factor?

One watt is equal to approximately 3.412142 BTU per hour.
