How Many Watts Is 50 Amps?

Reviewed by the ElectricalConverters editorial team Last updated July 2026 Estimates only — how we check
Quick Answer

A 50 A circuit at 120 V carries 6,000 W (4,800 W continuous), and at 240 V carries 12,000 W. Multiply amps × volts (× power factor for AC). Use the calculator below for any voltage.

RESULT
— W
Formula: W = A × V

Real example: a 50 A range, welder or RV service

50 A is the largest common residential branch rating — electric ranges, welders and 50 A RV pedestals, wired in 6 AWG copper. At 240 V that is 12,000 W (9,600 W continuous). At 120 V a 50 A circuit would be 6,000 W.

Frequently Asked Questions

How many watts is 50 amps at 120V?

At 120V, 50 A delivers 50 × 120 = 6,000 watts (resistive, PF 1). For a continuous load the NEC limits this to 80%: 4,800 W. For AC with a power factor below 1, multiply by PF.

How many watts is 50 amps at 240V?

At 240V, 50 A delivers 50 × 240 = 12,000 watts; the 80% continuous limit is 9,600 W.

How many watts can a 50 amp 240V circuit handle?

A 50 A, 240 V circuit delivers 50 × 240 = 12,000 W, or 9,600 W continuous. A 50 A RV pedestal actually provides two 120 V legs (2 × 50 A) for the same 12,000 W. For big single loads confirm wire and breaker with a licensed electrician.
