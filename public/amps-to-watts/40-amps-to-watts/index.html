How Many Watts Is 40 Amps?

Reviewed by the ElectricalConverters editorial team Last updated July 2026 Estimates only — how we check
Quick Answer

A 40 A circuit at 120 V carries 4,800 W (3,840 W continuous), and at 240 V carries 9,600 W. Multiply amps × volts (× power factor for AC). Use the calculator below for any voltage.

RESULT
— W
Formula: W = A × V

Real example: a 40 A range or Level 2 EV charger

40 A circuits (8 AWG copper) power electric ranges and Level 2 EV chargers. At 240 V a 40 A breaker carries 9,600 W; because an EV charger is a continuous load it is limited to 32 A / 7,680 W.

Frequently Asked Questions

How many watts is 40 amps at 120V?

At 120V, 40 A delivers 40 × 120 = 4,800 watts (resistive, PF 1). For a continuous load the NEC limits this to 80%: 3,840 W. For AC with a power factor below 1, multiply by PF.

How many watts is 40 amps at 240V?

At 240V, 40 A delivers 40 × 240 = 9,600 watts; the 80% continuous limit is 7,680 W.

How many watts is a 40 amp EV charger circuit?

A 40 A, 240 V circuit is rated 9,600 W, but an EV charger (a continuous load) may only use 80%: 7,680 W, i.e. 32 A. That is exactly why a "40 A" wallbox charges at 7.7 kW. Size the reverse — power to current — with kW to amps.
