How do you convert kilowatts to horsepower?

Divide kW by 0.7457 to get mechanical HP: HP = kW ÷ 0.7457. A 7.5 kW motor equals 7.5 ÷ 0.7457 = 10.06 HP. This is the reverse of the HP to kW conversion and is commonly used when comparing IEC-rated motors (kW) to NEMA-rated motors (HP).

Formula

HP = kW ÷ 0.7457

Where: HP = mechanical (imperial) horsepower, kW = kilowatts, 0.7457 = conversion factor (1 HP = 745.7 W = 0.7457 kW).

Horsepower types compared: Mechanical HP = 745.7 W (motors, most uses). Electrical HP = 746.0 W (US NEC nameplate). Metric HP (PS/CV) = 735.5 W (European vehicle engines). Boiler HP = 9,810.6 W (steam systems — unrelated to motor sizing).

Frequently Asked Questions

How many HP is 1 kW?

1 kW equals approximately 1.341 mechanical horsepower (1 ÷ 0.7457 = 1.3410). This is the standard conversion for electric motors.

How do you convert a 11 kW IEC motor to HP?

11 kW ÷ 0.7457 ≈ 14.75 HP. The nearest standard NEMA frame is 15 HP. IEC motors are typically specified in kW while NEMA motors use HP, making this conversion essential when sourcing replacements across standards.

What is the difference between mechanical and electrical horsepower?

Mechanical HP = 745.7 W; electrical HP = 746 W exactly. The difference is 0.04 % — negligible for practical use. For motor sizing, both are equivalent; use whichever your reference table specifies.

How do I convert HP back to kW?

Multiply HP by 0.7457: kW = HP × 0.7457. Use the HP to kW calculator for the forward conversion.
