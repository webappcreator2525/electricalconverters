How do you convert Wh to kWh?

Divide watt-hours by 1,000. A 500 Wh portable power station stores 500 ÷ 1,000 = 0.5 kWh. A 2,400 Wh battery equals 2.4 kWh. Because your utility bills in kilowatt-hours, converting Wh to kWh tells you exactly how much a full charge adds to your electricity meter and its running cost.

Wh to kWh Formula

kWh = Wh ÷ 1,000

A kilowatt-hour is simply 1,000 watt-hours — the same unit at a larger scale. One kWh is the energy of a 1,000 W appliance running for one hour. Dividing by 1,000 shifts the decimal three places, so 750 Wh becomes 0.75 kWh.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I convert Wh to kWh?

Divide the watt-hours by 1,000: kWh = Wh ÷ 1,000. For example, 500 Wh ÷ 1,000 = 0.5 kWh. A kilowatt-hour is 1,000 watt-hours, so you just move the decimal three places left.

How many kWh is 500 Wh?

500 Wh equals 0.5 kWh (500 ÷ 1,000). At 15¢ per kWh, recharging it costs roughly 7.5¢.

Is 1,000 Wh the same as 1 kWh?

Yes. The prefix "kilo" means one thousand, so 1,000 Wh = 1 kWh exactly.

Why convert watt-hours to kilowatt-hours?

Electricity is billed in kilowatt-hours, but batteries and appliances are often rated in watt-hours. Converting to kWh lets you compare against your bill and estimate running cost — pair it with our watts to kWh tool.
