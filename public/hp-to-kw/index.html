How do you convert horsepower to kilowatts?

Multiply HP by the conversion factor 0.7457: kW = HP × 0.7457. A 10 HP motor has a rated output of 10 × 0.7457 = 7.457 kW. This applies to the mechanical (imperial) horsepower where 1 hp = 745.7 W. Metric horsepower (PS/CV) is slightly smaller at 735.5 W; for metric HP use kW = PS × 0.7355.

Formula

kW = HP × 0.7457

Where: kW = kilowatts (mechanical output power), HP = mechanical (imperial) horsepower, 0.7457 = conversion factor (1 hp = 745.7 W = 0.7457 kW).

Note on variants: Metric horsepower (PS, CV, ch) = 735.5 W. Electrical horsepower = 746 W exactly (used in the US for motor nameplate ratings). The difference between metric and electrical HP is less than 0.1 %; for most practical calculations either factor is acceptable.

Frequently Asked Questions

How many kW is 1 HP?

1 horsepower (mechanical) equals 0.7457 kW or 745.7 watts. This is the internationally recognised conversion factor for motor power ratings.

What is the difference between mechanical HP and metric HP?

Mechanical (imperial) HP = 745.7 W, used in the US and UK. Metric HP (PS or CV) = 735.5 W, used in Europe for vehicle engines. For electric motors, the mechanical HP standard applies: kW = HP × 0.7457.

How many HP is 1 kW?

1 kW equals 1 ÷ 0.7457 ≈ 1.341 HP. Use the kW to HP calculator for the reverse conversion.

Does HP represent input or output power?

Motor HP ratings represent shaft output power — the mechanical power delivered to the load. Input power from the supply is higher because of motor losses; input kW = output kW ÷ efficiency. A 10 HP (7.457 kW output) motor at 92 % efficiency draws 7.457 ÷ 0.92 ≈ 8.1 kW from the supply.
