Frequently Asked Questions

How do I convert kVA to kW?

To convert kVA (apparent power) to kW (real power), multiply the kVA by the power factor. For example, 100 kVA at a power factor of 0.8 equals 80 kW.

What is the formula for kVA to kW?

The formula is: kW = kVA × PF, where kW is real power in kilowatts, kVA is apparent power in kilovolt-amps, and PF is the power factor (a number between 0 and 1).

What is power factor?

Power factor measures how efficiently electrical power is converted into useful work. It ranges from 0 to 1. Resistive loads (heaters) have a power factor of 1; inductive loads (motors) typically range from 0.7 to 0.95.

How many kW is 100 kVA at 0.8 PF?

At a power factor of 0.8, 100 kVA equals 80 kW. This is calculated as: 100 × 0.8 = 80.
