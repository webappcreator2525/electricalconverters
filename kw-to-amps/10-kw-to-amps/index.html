10 kW to Amps

Quick Answer

10 kW = 10,000 W. At 120 V DC: 83.3333 A. At 240 V DC: 41.6667 A. For AC circuits, divide by voltage × power factor (e.g. 240 V, PF 0.8: 52.0833 A). Use the calculator below for any voltage or circuit type.

RESULT — CURRENT
— A
Formula: A = (kW × 1,000) ÷ V

Frequently Asked Questions

How many amps is 10 kW at 240V?

10 kW at 240 V DC = (10 × 1,000) ÷ 240 = 41.6667 amps. For AC single-phase with PF 0.8: 10,000 ÷ (240 × 0.8) = 52.0833 amps.

How many amps is 10 kW at 120V?

10 kW at 120 V DC = (10 × 1,000) ÷ 120 = 83.3333 amps. For AC single-phase with PF 0.8: 10,000 ÷ (120 × 0.8) = 104.1667 amps. Loads of this magnitude are almost always served by 240 V or three-phase circuits.

What size breaker do I need for 10 kW?

At 240 V single-phase, PF 0.8: 10 kW draws 52.08 A. The NEC requires 125% for continuous loads: 52.08 × 1.25 = 65.1 A. A 70 A breaker is the correct choice. Use 4 AWG copper wire (rated 85 A). For three-phase at 480 V (PF 0.8): 15.03 A × 1.25 = 18.78 A — a 20 A breaker is adequate. Always consult a licensed electrician.
