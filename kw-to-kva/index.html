How do you convert kW to kVA?

Divide kW by the power factor: kVA = kW ÷ PF. An 8 kW load at PF 0.8 equals 8 ÷ 0.8 = 10 kVA. This is the inverse of the kVA to kW formula and is critical when sizing generators and UPS units, which are rated in kVA but loads are usually specified in kW.

Formula

kVA = kW ÷ PF

Where: kVA = apparent power in kilovolt-amps, kW = real power in kilowatts, PF = power factor (0–1).

Frequently Asked Questions

How do you convert kW to kVA?

Divide kW by the power factor: kVA = kW ÷ PF. For a 10 kW load at 0.85 PF, the result is 10 ÷ 0.85 ≈ 11.76 kVA.

What power factor should I use if I don't know my load's PF?

For mixed AC loads, 0.8 is a widely used conservative default. For motor-dominated loads, 0.85 is common. For data-centre UPS, manufacturers often assume 0.9 PF. Always check the equipment nameplate or data sheet when possible.

When is kW the same as kVA?

Only at PF = 1.0, which occurs with purely resistive loads (electric heaters, incandescent lamps). For all inductive or capacitive loads in AC circuits, kVA > kW.

How do I convert kVA back to kW?

Multiply kVA by the power factor: kW = kVA × PF. Use the kVA to kW calculator for the reverse conversion.
