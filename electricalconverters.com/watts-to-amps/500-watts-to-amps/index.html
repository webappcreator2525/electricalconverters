500 Watts to Amps

Quick Answer

At 120 V DC, 500 watts = 4.1667 A. At 240 V DC, 500 watts = 2.0833 A. For AC circuits, divide by voltage × power factor (e.g. at 120V AC, PF 0.8: 5.2083 A). Use the calculator below for any voltage or circuit type.

RESULT
— A
Formula: A = W ÷ V

Frequently Asked Questions

How many amps is 500 watts at 120V?

500 watts at 120V DC = 500 ÷ 120 = 4.1667 amps. For AC single-phase with PF 0.8: 500 ÷ (120 × 0.8) = 5.2083 amps. For AC three-phase with PF 0.8 at 120V L-N: 500 ÷ (120 × 0.8 × √3) = 3.0070 amps.

How many amps is 500 watts at 240V?

500 watts at 240V DC = 500 ÷ 240 = 2.0833 amps. For AC single-phase with PF 0.8: 500 ÷ (240 × 0.8) = 2.6042 amps.

What size breaker do I need for 500 watts?

At 120V, 500W draws 4.17A. The NEC requires breakers sized at 125% for continuous loads: 4.17 × 1.25 = 5.21A. The next standard size is a 15A breaker, which is the minimum residential standard and is more than sufficient. At 240V (2.08A), a 15A breaker is also adequate. Always consult a licensed electrician for circuit design.
